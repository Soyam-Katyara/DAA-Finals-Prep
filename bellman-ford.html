✅ Bellman-Ford

  • Works with negative edge weights
  • Works on directed graphs
  • Detects negative cycles
  • Simple to understand
  • Slower: O(V × E)

⚠ Dijkstra

  • Works on directed graphs
  • Faster: O((V+E) log V)
  • ❌ Breaks with negative edges
  • ❌ Cannot detect negative cycles
Why does Dijkstra fail with negative edges?
Dijkstra assumes: once a node is visited, its shortest distance is final. But a negative edge discovered later could make a path even shorter — breaking that assumption entirely. Bellman-Ford fixes this by checking every single edge, V−1 times, guaranteeing correctness even with negatives.
